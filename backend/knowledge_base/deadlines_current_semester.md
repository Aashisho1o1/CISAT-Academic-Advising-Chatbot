## What is "Regular Module" vs "Other Module"?

This is the single biggest source of deadline confusion for CISAT MS students.

**Regular Module** = Standard full-semester courses running the full 16 weeks.
Most CISAT courses are regular module.

**Other Module** = Everything else: weekend intensives, 8-week courses, January
interterm, summer sessions. These have MUCH shorter add/drop windows — sometimes
just 1-2 days.

**How to check which type your course is:** Log into the registration portal →
find your course → look for "Module" or "Session" field. If it says "Regular" or
"Full Term," it's regular module. Anything else is other module.

## Registration Deadlines

### Adding a Course — Regular Module (Full Semester)
- **Date:** [ENTER DATE]
- **Who this affects:** All MS students enrolling in regular 16-week courses
- **What this means in plain language:** This is the last day you can add a new
  full-semester course through the registration portal without special permission.
- **If you miss it:** You must submit a Late Add Petition. This requires approval
  from your program coordinator AND the Registrar's Office. Approval is not
  guaranteed. Contact your advisor immediately.
- **Important note:** This does NOT apply to intensive or weekend courses —
  those have separate, much shorter deadlines (see below).

### Adding a Course — Other Module (Intensive/Weekend/Accelerated)
- **Date:** Varies by course — typically 1-2 days after the course begins
- **Who this affects:** MS students enrolling in intensive, weekend, or
  accelerated format courses
- **What this means in plain language:** Because these courses are compressed
  (often 1-2 weekends or 8 weeks), the add deadline is very short. You may have
  only 1-2 days after the first class meeting to add.
- **If you miss it:** Same Late Add Petition process, but approval is less likely
  because you've already missed significant course content.
- **How to check your course's specific deadline:** Log into the registration
  portal → find your course → look for the "Module" or "Session" field and
  associated dates.

### Dropping a Course Without Record — Regular Module
- **Date:** [ENTER DATE]
- **Who this affects:** All MS students in regular module courses
- **What this means in plain language:** If you drop before this date, the course

- **If you miss it:** After this date, dropping results in a "W" (Withdrawal) on
  your transcript. A "W" does not affect your GPA but is visible on your transcript.

### Withdrawing from a Course (with "W" grade) — Regular Module
- **Date:** [ENTER DATE]
- **Who this affects:** All MS students in regular module courses
- **What this means in plain language:** Between the drop-without-record deadline
  and this date, you can leave the course but a "W" appears on your transcript.
- **If you miss it:** After this date, you cannot withdraw. You receive whatever
  grade you earn — or an F/Incomplete if you stop attending without arranging
  alternatives with the instructor.

## Emergency / Exception Information

If you've missed a deadline:
1. Don't panic — exceptions are sometimes possible
2. Contact your program coordinator immediately
3. Be prepared to explain your circumstances
4. Have any supporting documentation ready (medical, family emergency, etc.)
5. Act FAST — the sooner you reach out after missing a deadline, the better
   your chances of an exception
